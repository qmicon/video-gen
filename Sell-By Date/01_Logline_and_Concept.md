# Logline & Concept

## Working title
**SELL-BY DATE** — a pun on "sell-by date" + selling *by date* (era).

## Format
- **90-second vertical (9:16)** episodic short-film series.
- Each episode is **self-contained** (one modern product, one historical era, a complete story) but shares a **recurring crew + world**, plus a **serialized thread** (the timeline slowly degrading; how close they are to erasing themselves).
- Model: *Viva La Dirt League / Epic NPC Man* (self-contained comedic stories in one shared world) + micro-drama retention mechanics (hook in 3s, turn/cliffhanger at the end).

## Logline `[StudioBinder Ep4 — Formula A + specificity]`
> **When their ad agency goes broke, a silver-tongued closer and his two misfits discover they can sell modern products to people in the past — and race to cash in on history's untapped markets before changing the timeline too much erases them from existence.**

- *Inciting incident:* agency goes broke / discovers the ability.
- *Protagonist:* Drew, the silver-tongued closer (the catalyst the crew revolves around).
- *Action:* sell modern products to the past.
- *Antagonist + stake (fused):* over-change the timeline = self-erasure.

## Controlling Idea `[McKee]`
> **"Anything can be sold to anyone — but every sale rewrites the world, because ambition always outruns conscience."**

The greed and the threat are the same engine — which is what makes it a *series*, not a premise.

## Core value swing
**Existence ↔ erasure** (with **control ↔ chaos** underneath). The harder they sell, the closer they come to deleting themselves.

## Tone `[StudioBinder Ep5 — "X meets Y"]`
> **Mad Men meets Rick and Morty — staged in a Blackadder court.**

Ad-man swagger and the *feeling*-not-the-product pitch (Mad Men) × irreverent sci-fi comedy with real stakes (Rick and Morty) × witheringly witty period characters (Blackadder).

## Premise (one paragraph)
A slick-but-chaotic startup ad agency, days from folding, stumbles onto a way to reach into the past. Instead of dying quietly, they treat history as the ultimate untapped market: each episode they pick a modern product, target an era, and sell it. But the past has an immune system — change it too much and the ripple erases them from the present. Every sale is both the joke and the consequence.

## The 3 keystone decisions
1. **Origin:** accidental discovery (failing agency pivots to survive).
2. **Antagonist:** self-erasure through overreach (their ambition is the enemy).
3. **Cast:** three-hander, **Drew = clear solo lead**.
