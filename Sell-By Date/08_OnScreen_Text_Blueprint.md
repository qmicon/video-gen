# SELL-BY DATE — On-Screen Text Blueprint

The spec every on-screen caption is checked against. Built from `../hooks.md` + viral short-form hook research (vexub.com 25-formula library) + the pattern that tested strongest for us.

---

## 0. Why on-screen text decides everything
- Most feed viewing is **on mute** — the caption carries the hook for audio-off viewers.
- It's a **distribution lever**: videos holding 60%+ of viewers past 3 seconds get pushed to For You / Reels / Shorts.
- The first-frame caption is the **second hook**, firing alongside the visual.

## 1. The laws (every caption passes all)
1. **Lands in the first 3 seconds. ≤14 words** (aim under 12).
2. **Stacks ≥2 triggers:** curiosity gap · pattern interrupt · self-relevance · emotional arousal. (Single-trigger captions underperform.)
3. **Completes the visual — never labels it.** Ban "Drew's first sales call."
4. **Contains ONE concrete number** — the specificity multiplier (the **$20K** move).
5. **Opens a loop** the brain must close (the "how / why").
6. **Mute test:** caption + frame alone must deliver the hook.

## 2. The house formulas (our narrative-comedy subset)
We don't use all 25 viral formulas — most are for talking-head content. We use this tight set:

### HOOK CAPTION — "First-Person Impossible Result" (our signature)
> **Template:** `I [went to ERA] and sold [PRODUCT] for [REWARD] ($CONCRETE VALUE).`
- First person (Drew's POV) = story pull.
- Impossible result = the absurd outcome stated as fact.
- Concrete $ figure = the multiplier.
- **Locked Ep1:** `I went back in time and sold an energy drink for a bag of gold ($20K).`

### END CARD — "Cliffhanger Question" (the next-episode hook)
> **Template:** `Next: how do you sell [PRODUCT] to [ABSURD BUYER]?`
- **Example:** `Next: how do you sell sunscreen to a sun-worshipping empire?`

### (Optional) MID CARD — used at most once, only for a real mid-point twist. Same laws.

## 3. The specificity rule (non-negotiable)
Every hook caption names a **concrete number**. "A bag of gold" → **$20K**. Numbers are the single biggest performance multiplier. No vague rewards ("a fortune," "a lot of money," "and it changed everything").

## 4. Formatting
- `$20K` — never `(20k$)`. Clean, fast-read.
- ALL-CAPS one key word for punch when useful ("sold **ONE** energy drink").
- Big, high-contrast, 1–2 lines, readable on mute.

## 5. The 3-variant rule
Never ship one caption. Write **3 per episode** and A/B them — the same video with a different caption can 5x reach. Rotate formulas so the audience can't pattern-skip the opener.

## 6. Banned
- Labels that explain the scene ("Drew's first sales call").
- Vague rewards/outcomes.
- Over 14 words, or burying the hook behind context.
- Captions that just narrate what's already on screen.

## 7. Caption bank (plug-and-play)

**Ep1 — battlefield / energy drink (hook variants):**
- `I went back in time and sold an energy drink for a bag of gold ($20K).`
- `A dying warlord paid me $20K in gold for one energy drink.`
- `I sold ONE energy drink in the past. It paid $20,000.`

**Ep1 — end card:**
- `Next: how do you sell sunscreen to a sun-worshipping empire?`

**Ep2 — Aztecs / sunscreen (hook variants, draft):**
- `I sold sunscreen to a sun god and got paid $40K in gold.`
- `A sun-worshipping empire paid me a fortune to block the sun. ($40K)`

## 8. Pre-ship checklist
- ≤14 words, lands in 3s?
- ≥2 triggers firing?
- One concrete number?
- Completes (not labels) the visual?
- Opens a loop?
- 3 variants written?
