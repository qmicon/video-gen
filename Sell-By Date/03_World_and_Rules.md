# World & Rules

*Kept deliberately light — the device is a means, not the subject.*

## The device
- The agency can **reach into the past** and operate there (in the Ep1 draft, Drew physically steps through a portal in the loft).
- How it was acquired: **accidental discovery** during the agency's collapse (exact mechanism kept vague on purpose — comedy lives in the *selling*, not the sci-fi).
- Optional later layer: the device can pair to a **robot/proxy body** for remote operation in another era (original seed idea; parked unless an episode needs it).

## The selling mechanic (per episode)
1. **Pick a modern product** (= the placed brand / ad inventory).
2. **Target an era** where it could plausibly "sell" — the *match* is the differentiator and the comedy.
3. **Sell it** — culture clash, persuasion, the pitch.

## The antagonist engine — "history's immune system"
- Change the past **too much** and the ripple **erases the crew** from the present.
- **Margo watches the "erasure meter."** Every aggressive close moves it toward red.
- Core value swing per episode: **existence ↔ erasure**. Drew's hamartia ("close at any cost") is the throttle.
- For the pitch, this can later get a **human face** (a recurring "fixer" who shows up when they've gone too far) — but the core antagonist is the erasure risk itself.

## Product-placement model (ties to ViralAdsGen business)
- The **product is the story**, not background dressing → premium ad inventory.
- **Present-day is the placement hub** (real brands sit naturally); the **past** is where the product is *sold*; the **button** shows the brand rewritten into history (e.g., a billboard that now reads "[BRAND] — since 1684").
- A real advertiser fills the `[BRAND]` slot per episode.

## The 90-second beat sheet (every episode)
| Time | Beat | Job |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:05 | **Hook** | Drop us mid-action: "We're selling [product] in [era]." |
| 0:05–0:35 | **The match + obstacle** | Why this era; why it's hard (culture clash) |
| 0:35–1:05 | **Escalation** | The plan goes sideways — comedy compounds (but / therefore) |
| 1:05–1:20 | **The turn** | It works too well / backfires / changes history |
| 1:20–1:30 | **Button + brand** | Payoff line + product beat + mini-cliffhanger to next episode |

**Causality rule `[Ep3, South Park]`:** beats connect with *therefore / but*, never *and then*.
