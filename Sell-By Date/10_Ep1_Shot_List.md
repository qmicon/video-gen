## The key shots — director's deep-dive (stacked recipes)

**#1–3 · The Hook** `[01][06][07][08]` — Drop us mid-action. #1: ground-level + fisheye + handheld + step-print = drugged, disoriented arrival; HOOK caption fires (mute-readable). #2: crane boom-down establishing for era + scale, depth-layered for 4:3, burning banner as the bright focal point. #3–4: he turns in circles (objective) + his POV-whip of the chaos (subjective) = denial. **#6–7 · Sword at the throat (Power)** `[03][07][10]` — The grab plays in a wide handheld `1S→2S` (the Commander invades Drew's frame); **cut-on-action on the grab** carries us to a low-angle OTS over Drew's shoulder where **the sword swings across the throat**, then the interrogation holds on a slow push — the Commander towers, vertical lines dominate, blade glinting close to the lens. **#9–12 · The denial dies → the closer wakes up** `[04][06]` — The engine of the new scene. #9 racks past the Commander to a *real* spear-death in the background (the thing that makes it real). #11 is Drew's horror at eye-level, static-trapped. #12 is the turn — a slow push on an 85mm as his eyes shift from terror to calculation. We watch the salesman boot up under the fear.

**#13–16 · The pitch + product hero** `[01][07]` — Drew flips to merchant; the handheld steadies as control returns (camera mirrors his recovery). #15 low-angle as he regains power, holding the can up like a relic; #16 is the hero insert of the VAGE label — the placed ad, sold *in-story*.

**#29 · The kill (tonal pivot)** `[08][09]` — Slow-mo blood spray (horror made comic) → smash cut to near-silence, static hold so we can't look away; blood red is the only saturated thing against the mud.

**#41 · The money shot** `[04][08][11]` — Overcranked gold cascade, rack pouch→face, slow pedestal-down reveal. **Gold = the brightest, warmest object in frame** `[11]`. The product-value image — it sells the premise.

**#45 · The conversion (dolly-zoom)** `[16]` — The single most important shot. Dolly-in + zoom-out, push-more-than-zoom (Squid Game), MS→CU; background stretches while Drew stays fixed — the world reorganizes around "twenty grand." **#58 · The button** `[09]` — Dip-to-black for a beat, then the cliffhanger-question end card that hooks Ep2.

## Production notes

1. **Graphic-violence sensitivity (was: profanity):** the rewrite trades "What the fuck" for **on-screen violence** — sword at the throat, a blood bead on the neck (#8), a soldier speared and dropping (#9), blood spray (#29). Meta/TikTok may suppress/demonetize visible blood + death. Plan coverage: a **softer cut** (imply the spear-death in soft background or off-frame, reduce blood volume, quick on the neck) **and** a bolder uncensored cut. Decide per platform at edit. *(NB: the script file's own bottom note still references "What the fuck," which is no longer in the dialogue — flag to update the script doc.)*
2. **Causality** `[03/South Park]`: beats connect *therefore/but*. The real death (#9) *forces* the belief (#11) *therefore* the pitch (#13); it-works (#24) *but* the kill (#29) *forces* the flee (#31–32); the gold (#41) *forces* the math (#45) *therefore* the greed (#48).
3. **Frame-rate discipline** `[08]`: only tagged SLO/RAMP/UNDER shots leave 24fps. Overcrank = high-fps capture, undercrank = low-fps. Don't mix elsewhere.
4. **Portal continuity:** same glowing-tear asset + screen position in #1, #32, #37, #52; cold cyan ≈6000K spill on nearby faces/floor.
5. **Drew state:** blood **on the neck** from #8, **across the face** from #29; mud-caked from #1 through the entire loft scene.
6. **4:3 framing** `[11]`: compose with depth (fg/mid/bg), center-block the key subject (eye-trace), gold/banner as the brightest object, rule-of-odds on 3-crew shots.
7. **Lighting practicals-only** `[12]`, camera from the shadow side — carry into the Seedance prompts.
8. **Sound is the top lever** `[19][21]`: battle bed, the smash-to-silence on #29, and the score's it-works (#24) → panic → greed (#41–48) turns carry more than any single visual. Cut bg ~1200Hz under Margo's warning (#54) so dread creeps.
9. **Runtime:** the rewritten Scene 1 is now a full pitch scene (~0:55) — total likely **~1:45–2:00**, over the 1:30 title. Either retitle, or trim the compressible parts (the interrogation #7 and the merchant pitch #13 can tighten). Flag for the writing pass.
