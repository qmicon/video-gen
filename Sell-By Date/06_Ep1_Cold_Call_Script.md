# EP 1 — "COLD CALL"

*Spec screenplay (per `../spec-screenplay.md`), ~90 sec, vertical. Dialogue checked against `07_Dialogue_Blueprint.md`; on-screen text against `08_OnScreen_Text_Blueprint.md`. Self-contained episode — not a "pilot."*

**Structure:** accidental first sale → period customer pays huge → customer killed mid-reorder → Drew flees in panic → the margin lands, panic flips to greed → he aims at a new, safer market and the series engine launches.

---

```
TITLE: SELL-BY DATE — EP 1 — "COLD CALL" — 1:30
DURATION: 1:30
TONAL REGISTER: Absurd / loud and physical (action-comedy)
LOGLINE: A broke ad man trips through a rip in time into a
  medieval battle, accidentally sells an energy drink to a
  warlord who's killed mid-reorder, and learns the past will
  pay anything.
PROTAGONIST: Drew — washed-up ad closer. Want: a sale that
  saves the agency.
CONVERSION BEAT: The margin landing — $200 of cans for $20K
  in gold — flipping his panic to greed.
CTA: End card; the agency aims at a new market; next target teased.

FADE IN.

EXT. MUDDY BATTLEFIELD - DAY

Drew tumbles out of a glowing tear and face-plants in the mud.
Clashing steel, screaming men, a burning banner. He still grips
a can of energy drink.

ON SCREEN: I went back in time and sold an energy drink
           for a bag of gold ($20K).

A horse THUNDERS past, inches from his head.

                    DREW
          What the fuck.

A huge ARMORED COMMANDER — bloodied, gasping — grabs Drew by the
collar, rips the can from his hand, and SHOVES him down. Drew
lands hard on his backside in the mud.

The Commander looms over him, holding up the can.

                    COMMANDER
          What is this?

                    DREW
          (from the ground)
          ...Energy drink.

The Commander chugs it. His eyes snap wide open. He tears a
pouch from his belt and hurls it — Drew catches it against his
chest.

                    COMMANDER
          More. Bring me a hundred. Bring me—

A RIDER sweeps through. A blade flashes. Blood sprays across
Drew's face. The Commander drops.

Drew freezes, dripping. Then scrambles backward through the mud,
staggers up, and dives headfirst into the glowing tear.

INT. AGENCY LOFT - CONTINUOUS

Drew crashes onto the floor — mud-caked, blood across his face,
still clutching the pouch. KAI and MARGO jump up.

                    MARGO
          What the hell happened?

                    DREW
          (scrambling back from the tear)
          Close it — close it!

Kai hits a key. The glowing tear shrinks to a seam and snaps shut.

Drew sits in the mud he tracked in, chest heaving.

                    MARGO
          Whose blood is that?

                    DREW
          Not mine. I think.

He looks down at the pouch. Tips it. Gold coins pour into his
lap. Everyone stares.

                    DREW (CONT'D)
          A warlord ordered a hundred energy
          drinks. That's the deposit.

                    MARGO
          A warlord?

                    DREW
          He's dead now. Long story.

                    MARGO
          I don't understand.

                    DREW
          (the math landing)
          A hundred cans costs me two hundred
          bucks. He left twenty grand.

                    KAI
          ...In gold.

It lands on all three of them. Drew stands, grabs a full case
of cans.

                    DREW
          Kai. Open it back up.

                    KAI
          You just told me to close it.

                    DREW
          Yeah — somewhere else this time. Some
          other century. Not a battlefield.

Kai swivels to the rig and starts dialing it up. Margo flattens
her hand over the keyboard.

                    MARGO
          Stop. Every product we drop back there
          is a perturbation. Compound enough and
          the timeline re-resolves — this present
          gets overwritten. Maybe without us in it.

                    DREW
          Then we get rich before it does.
          (to Kai)
          Somewhere hot, holy, and loaded.

Kai grins, fingers flying.

ON SCREEN: SELL-BY DATE
           Next: how do you sell sunscreen to a
           sun-worshipping empire?

FADE OUT.

CAST:
DREW — washed-up ad closer; the protagonist.
KAI — young engineer; opened the rip.
MARGO — historian; tracks the damage, speaks in jargon.
COMMANDER — a battlefield warlord; the (very brief) first customer.
RIDER — an enemy horseman; ends the sale.
```
