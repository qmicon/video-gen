# References (research log)

Everything we surveyed, sorted by what job it does. Tiering reflects the final format (90-second narrative episodic series), not the early long-form ideas.

## Tone (funny + real stakes)
- **Rick and Morty** — the tonal target: irreverent comedy with genuine stakes/heart + scheme-of-the-week structure.
- **Back to the Future** — clean, satisfying time-causality the audience can follow (our "rules" benchmark).
- **Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure** — lovable, warm, era-clash comedy (not cynical).
- **Gintama** — masterclass in comedy that pivots to real stakes when it wants.

## Format model (short-form narrative series)
- **Viva La Dirt League — "Epic NPC Man"** — *the* creative model: self-contained comedic mini-stories in one shared world with recurring characters. Channel: youtube.com/channel/UCchBatdUMZoMfJ3rIzgV84g
- **Vertical micro-dramas (ReelShort, DramaBox)** — retention mechanics only (3-second hook, complete beat per episode, cliffhanger). Ignore their melodrama tone.
- **Horrible Histories — sketches & fake adverts** — historical comedy at sketch length; the closest precedent for "products sold in the past." youtube.com playlist PLRlbIiRJTXx_reLX78G0Rpwxkp1vD1pq1

## Cross-cultural trade / negotiation craft (mine for authenticity, not as watches)
- **Shōgun (2024)** — outsider learns a culture and turns it into leverage; production-design benchmark.
- **Spice and Wolf** — how to dramatize a *negotiation*; medieval economics.
- **Ascendance of a Bookworm** — modern know-how → build a business → become a supplier inside an old economy.
- **Tai-Pan (novel)** / **Taboo (Peacock)** — founding a trading house / trader vs. establishment.
- **A Bride's Story (manga)** — visual mood-board fuel (19th-c. Silk Road).

## Real-history episode seeds (the strongest, most original well)
- **Vasco da Gama, Calicut 1498** — brought cheap gifts to a rich king; court laughed. → "know your customer."
- **Macartney Embassy, China 1793** — protocol clash + emperor "we have no use for your manufactures." → "the customer doesn't even want it."
- **East India Company "factory" system** — resident agents ("factors") embedded long-term. → the historical version of "send an agent to live there."
- **Dejima (Dutch in Japan)** — trade allowed only under absurd restrictions. → "operate under the host's rules."

## What NOT to do (the existing "vibe ad" accounts — no story)
These prove the AI-vintage-ad appetite but are aesthetic loops with **no characters or plot** — our differentiation is *story + recurring crew + the "right timeline" logic*:
- **AdTime Machine** (YouTube) — "What if [modern product] existed in [era]" vintage AI commercials.
- **@retro.studios** (TikTok) — AI retro ads with real brands.
- IG examples: instagram.com/reel/DXt-GFEEsbK/ (iPhone 1955), instagram.com/reel/DYFmu8SM8my/ (ChatGPT late-90s).
