# Episode Seeds

Goal `[Ep5]`: 5–6 episode ideas that prove the show "could go on forever." Each = one product × one era × one trade-problem, ending on a history-ripple turn.

## EP 1 — "The Sweet Smell of Success" *(developed → see 06)*
- **Product:** deodorant. **Era:** Versailles, court of Louis XIV.
- **Trade problem:** *the customer doesn't think he has a problem* (odor = status). (Macartney logic.)
- **Turn:** Drew reframes it as exclusivity; the King buys "all of it"; the entire French perfume industry is erased from the present.

## EP 2 — "Burn Notice" *(teased in Ep1 button)*
- **Product:** sunscreen. **Era:** Aztec empire.
- **Trade problem:** *religious/moral objection* — their god IS the sun; blocking it reads as heresy.
- **Possible turn:** it becomes a sacred relic; sun-worship reorganizes around SPF.

## EP 3 — "First Impressions" (da Gama seed)
- **Product:** a "premium" modern gift basket / luxury sampler. **Era:** a rich 1490s spice port.
- **Trade problem:** *gift/etiquette protocol* — the "premium" offering insults a king who trades in gold and jewels.

## EP 4 — "Lost in Translation"
- **Product:** anything with a slogan. **Era:** any court with no shared language.
- **Trade problem:** *language barrier* — a mistranslated pitch sells the wrong thing entirely, with consequences.

## EP 5 — "The Only Vendor" (Dejima seed)
- **Product:** TBD. **Era:** an isolationist regime that allows one foreign stall under surveillance.
- **Trade problem:** *restricted access / licensing* — humiliating terms, but the upside of being the only licensed seller.

## EP 6 — "Going Native" (the factor seed)
- **Beat:** the crew plants an embedded agent who stops reporting back and joins the locals.
- **Trade problem:** *the agent goes native* — divided loyalty; the embed becomes a competitor.

## The trade-problem toolkit (obstacle generator)
Each is a reusable episode engine, drawn from real history:
| Problem | Comedic version |
|---|---|
| Gift/etiquette protocol | "Premium" offering insults the host |
| Buyer sees no value | The era doesn't want/understand the product |
| Language & translation | Mistranslated pitch sells the wrong thing |
| Local gatekeepers/middlemen | A guild/official demands a cut |
| Restricted access / licensing | Sell only from one watched stall |
| Payment & trust | What currency? Barter? Credit to people who've never heard of it? |
| Religious/moral objection | Product offends belief; crew branded heretics |
| The agent goes native | Embedded agent joins the locals |
